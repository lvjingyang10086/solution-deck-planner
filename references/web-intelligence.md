# Public Web Intelligence

Use this module during project-intelligence intake when a target organization is identifiable and public context could improve the briefing.

## Confirm the research brief

Before browsing, confirm or infer with low risk:

- Exact organization, brand, parent/subsidiary, region, and industry
- Relevant executives and roles
- Default time range: latest 2-3 years, plus older material only when strategically important
- Topics: AI, digitalization, intelligent transformation, data, cloud, automation, innovation, operating model, investment, and strategic targets
- Intended use: background understanding, opening narrative, solution alignment, stakeholder preparation, or evidence

Show a concise search plan and obtain confirmation unless the user explicitly requested web research.

## Search in layers

Prioritize primary and authoritative sources:

1. Official website, strategy pages, newsroom, annual reports, ESG reports, investor materials, and procurement notices
2. Official social accounts, verified interviews, conference speeches, earnings calls, and executive articles
3. Government, regulator, exchange, industry-association, and academic sources
4. Reputable media and credible partner/customer announcements

Use third-party summaries to discover leads, then trace consequential claims to the original source whenever possible. Search in the organization's native language and English when useful.

## Query matrix

Combine the organization name and executive names with:

- AI, artificial intelligence, generative AI, large model, agent
- digital strategy, digital transformation, intelligent transformation
- data strategy, cloud, automation, innovation, productivity
- annual report, strategy target, investment, roadmap, initiative
- speech, interview, keynote, forum, earnings call, signed article
- partnership, pilot, platform, organizational change, talent

Iterate queries based on discovered program names, slogans, platforms, executives, and subsidiaries.

## Extract usable intelligence

Capture:

- Organization overview relevant to the briefing
- Stated strategic priorities and measurable targets
- AI/digital initiatives, platforms, partnerships, investments, and milestones
- Executive statements with speaker, title, date, event, and direct source
- Organizational, capability, or governance signals
- Current constraints, risks, or contradictions visible in public information
- Customer terminology, slogans, and named programs
- Implications for the presentation, clearly labeled as analysis rather than fact

Use short quotes only when the exact wording materially improves the briefing. Prefer paraphrase and always link the source.

## Public intelligence brief

Return:

| Intelligence | Type | Date | Source | Confidence | Relevance to briefing |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Then summarize:

1. Three to five strategic signals
2. Relevant executive viewpoints
3. AI/intelligent-transformation actions and targets
4. Opportunities to align the solution narrative
5. Potential contradictions or issues requiring verification
6. Recommended facts for main slides versus appendix/background only

## Integrity rules

- Cite every consequential or time-sensitive claim with a direct link.
- Preserve publication date and, where different, event date.
- Distinguish `public fact`, `executive statement`, `third-party report`, and `analytical implication`.
- Do not infer private intent, budget, personal stance, or procurement status from weak signals.
- Do not present old strategy as current without checking for updates.
- Cross-check high-impact claims with at least two credible sources when practical.
- Never fabricate quotations or merge separate statements into a synthetic quote.
