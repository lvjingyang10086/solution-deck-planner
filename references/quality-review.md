# Quality Review

## Scorecard

Score out of 100:

- Decision goal clarity: 15
- Narrative completeness: 20
- Audience fit: 15
- Evidence sufficiency: 15
- Solution response/closure: 15
- Page expressibility: 10
- Permission and risk control: 10

Below 80, present priority corrections before declaring a final version.

## Objection preparation

For each high-risk question record audience, question, response direction, proof, main/appendix page, demo need, and remaining gap. Prioritize:

- EB: urgency, ROI, duplication, failure risk, supplier choice.
- TB: integration, permissions, security, model error, deployment, reliability, maintainability, exit risk.
- Business: workflow realism, adoption effort, responsibility, automation boundary, measurement.

## Final gate

Do not declare a final version unless:

- Core claim, audience, success criterion, and requested action are confirmed.
- Chapter order and page budget are coherent.
- Important claims have adequate evidence or cautious labels.
- No critical source conflict remains unresolved.
- Sensitive information has an approved treatment.
- No critical page has a content, evidence, or permission gap.
- Structured production fields are complete.

## Exceptions

- **Insufficient information**: ask at most three high-impact questions and state working assumptions.
- **Information overload**: prioritize by decision relevance; put the rest in a reference pool.
- **Conflicting objectives**: recommend a primary objective and move secondary goals to appendix or a separate deck.
- **Unsupported claim**: weaken the wording, request proof, or convert the page to a validation plan.
- **Inaccessible source**: disclose the limitation; never imply it was read.
- **Weak existing deck**: classify as local edit, reorder, rebuild, or audience split before editing the outline.
