# Permission and Source Policy

Apply these principles to files, knowledge bases, conversations, customer materials, and external sources.

1. Inherit the current user's role, department, and visible data scope.
2. Separate analysis access from presentation permission.
3. Do not expose information across departments, customers, or confidentiality levels without explicit authorization.
4. Treat pricing, personnel opinions, competitive strategy, customer identity/data, and unannounced capabilities as sensitive by default.
5. Use redaction, aggregation, placeholders, internal-only speaker notes, or exclusion when needed.
6. Preserve provenance for material claims: source, date, confidence, and evidence grade.
7. Respect original source permissions; do not modify project knowledge or source systems.
8. Keep tool use and source selection traceable.

When a restricted item is analytically useful but cannot be shown, explain the implication without revealing the protected content and mark the slide `RESTRICTED` or `CONFIRM`.
