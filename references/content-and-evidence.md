# Content Master and Evidence

## Content master

Organize reusable content into: context/trends, customer situation, problems/impact, key judgment, solution, company/product capabilities, business scenarios, value/proof, implementation/governance, differentiation, and requested action.

Tag each item with source, date, audience, confidentiality, evidence grade, page/appendix suitability, customer problem, and version status.

## Evidence grades

- **A - Verified fact**: confirmed customer data, formal documents, test results, approved cases.
- **B - Reliable basis**: maintained product material, public authoritative research, confirmed meeting record.
- **C - Analytical inference**: reasonable analysis with an explicit basis.
- **D - Unverified assumption**: requires validation and must not be stated as fact.

## Solution closure map

Create a table with:

`customer problem | root cause | solution capability | scenario/workflow | business value | evidence | owner/status`

Move unrelated capabilities to the appendix. Flag unanswered major problems and unsupported value claims.

## Conflicts

When sources disagree, show the conflicting statements and sources, assess decision impact, recommend a preferred interpretation with reasons, and request confirmation. Until confirmed, use a placeholder or cautious wording.

## Page budget

Use meeting time as a starting point, then adjust for interaction and complexity:

- 15 minutes: about 8-12 main pages
- 30 minutes: about 15-20 main pages
- 45 minutes: about 20-30 main pages
- Technical reviews: concise main narrative plus targeted technical appendix

Require every added page to answer a decision question or supply necessary proof.
