# Slide Brief Schema

## Management view

Show this first:

| Page | Conclusion-led title | Persuasion task | Primary audience | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## Production brief

For every approved page provide:

- `number`
- `title`: state a conclusion, not merely a topic
- `purpose`
- `primary_audience`
- `cognitive_change`
- `key_message`
- `supporting_points`: normally 3-5
- `evidence`: source and status
- `layout`: spatial composition and reading order
- `visual_type`: comparison, process, architecture, matrix, timeline, funnel, loop, case, or decision
- `visual_focus`: first thing the audience should notice
- `assets`: existing, to find, to generate, or customer-provided
- `speaker_outline`: opening, emphasis, and transition; do not write a full script by default
- `permissions`
- `readiness_status`

## Title rules

Prefer “topic + judgment” or a direct conclusion. Example: replace “企业AI落地难点” with “企业AI落地的核心障碍，正在从技术能力转向组织承接”.

## Density

- EB: one judgment per page; prioritize numbers, proof, and decisions.
- TB: higher density is acceptable when standards, boundaries, and verification remain readable.
- CIO: balance strategy, architecture, governance, and value.
- Business expert: prioritize workflow, roles, scenarios, and measures.
- Technical expert: allow detailed diagrams with an explicit reading path.

## Readiness statuses

Use `READY`, `CONTENT-GAP`, `EVIDENCE-GAP`, `ASSET-GAP`, `CONFIRM`, `RESTRICTED`, or `APPENDIX`.
