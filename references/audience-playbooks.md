# Audience Playbooks

## Stakeholder model

For each important participant identify decision power, current stance, core concern, main objection, evidence preference, and desired movement. Use stance values: strong supporter, inclined supporter, neutral, questioning, opposing, or not engaged.

## CIO

Emphasize strategic alignment, enterprise architecture, governance, capability reuse, investment path, organizational ownership, security, and measurable outcomes. Balance business and technology.

## EB - Economic Buyer

The EB has final funding approval and can make the purchase decision without further escalation. Emphasize business urgency, value, cost of inaction, investment logic, implementation confidence, risk control, comparable proof, and an explicit decision request. Reduce low-level technical detail.

## TB - Technical Buyer

The TB establishes technical selection standards and has recommendation power plus technical veto power. Emphasize standards fit, architecture, integration, data and permission boundaries, security, deployment, reliability, observability, maintainability, validation, and unacceptable risks.

## Technical expert

Provide mechanisms, interfaces, constraints, performance evidence, failure handling, security design, test results, and clear technical boundaries. Avoid unsupported abstractions.

## Business expert

Center the current workflow, role pain, future workflow, human-AI collaboration, exceptions, adoption burden, business measures, and scenario realism. Use business language before platform terminology.

## Mixed audience

Identify the primary decision maker. Keep shared decision content in the main narrative; move detailed technical or business proof to targeted appendix pages and verbal responses.

## Adaptation matrix

For each content item mark one of: `retain`, `compress`, `rewrite`, `remove`, `appendix`. Preserve factual consistency across versions.
